    About SEMD 2026

    South East Migration Dialogue 2026

    Register Free

    The South East Migration Dialogue (SEMD) 2026 is a strategic, high-level intervention designed to domesticate Nigeria's National Migration Policy at the sub-national (state) level. It aims to shift the narrative from "stopping migration" to "enhancing migration" for development.

    Read More

    The 2025 Revised National Migration Policy introduces a fourth level of governance - state-level coordination structure, providing a framework for formally integrating state and local actors into the national migration governance structure.

    Key Facilitators

    Distinguished leaders steering the South East Migration Dialogue 2026

    Mrs. Priscilia Otti

    Chief Host - Wife of the Governor of Abia State

    As Chief Host of SEMD 2026, Her Excellency provides distinguished patronage and support for the South East Migration Dialogue, demonstrating the state government's commitment to addressing migration governance and development issues in the region. Through her "Nneoma Foundation," she has championed skills acquisition programs that equip young people with employable skills, reducing the push factors of irregular migration and creating safe migration pathways for Abia State residents.

    Dr. Charles Ukauwa

    Chairman, Local Organizing Committee - Abia State

    Leading the strategic direction and coordination of the South East Migration Dialogue, Dr. Ukuwa ensures effective stakeholder engagement and successful implementation of the dialogue objectives. With extensive experience in civil society governance, he has been pivotal in securing high-level political buy-in for the dialogue and aligning all activities with the 2025 National Migration Policy framework.

    Rev. Fr. Emeka Obiezu

    National Coordinator, Civil Society Network on Migration and Development (CSOnetMADE)

    Rev. Fr. Emeka Obiezu is the National Coordinator of CSOnetMADE, Nigeria's premier civil society network on migration governance. An ordained Catholic priest, he brings a profound moral compass and pastoral commitment to the protection of human dignity in migration. Under his leadership, CSOnetMADE has grown into a robust nationwide coalition spanning all six geopolitical zones, with active state-level coordinators and thematic working groups on labour migration, counter-trafficking, returns and reintegration, and diaspora engagement. He has been instrumental in positioning civil society as a critical stakeholder in Nigeria's National Migration Policy, facilitating high-level dialogues between government agencies, international partners, and grassroots communities. Rev. Fr. Obiezu champions the domestication of migration governance at the sub-national level, safe and regular migration pathways, and the socio-economic reintegration of returnees.
